\section{Scope and conventions}\label{sec:prelim-scope}

This chapter fixes the geometric and spectral conventions used throughout the
monograph.  The subsequent construction of localized kernels, spectral projectors,
truncation identities, and trace expansions will refer to the present chapter for
normalizations, rather than reintroducing them ad hoc.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Modules.}
For ease of citation the background is organized into three self-contained blocks:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item \emph{Geometry} (Block \texttt{02b-geometry}): the hyperbolic model, metric,
  measure, distance, and the Laplacian convention on $\mathbb H$ and on the quotient
  $M=\Gamma\backslash\mathbb H$, together with standard identities used later.
  \item \emph{Cusps and truncation} (Block \texttt{02b-cusps}): scaling matrices,
  cusp widths, truncation domains $M(Y)$, Fourier expansions at cusps, Maass--Selberg
  relations, and the normalization of Eisenstein series and scattering.
  \item \emph{Selberg/Harish--Chandra transform} (Block \texttt{02b-selberg-transform}):
  transform conventions for radial kernels on $\mathbb H$, inversion and Plancherel,
  and the test-function classes appearing in trace identities.
\end{itemize}

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{References.}
Our conventions are compatible with the classical treatments in
Selberg~\cite{Selberg1956}, Hejhal~\cite{Hejhal1983}, Buser~\cite{Buser1992},
and Iwaniec~\cite{Iwaniec2002}.  Whenever a later statement depends on a specific
normalization choice, it is traced back to the corresponding block cited above.

\section{Asymptotic notation and admissible dependence}\label{sec:prelim-asymp}

We write $A\lesssim B$ if $A\le C B$ for a constant $C>0$ independent of the
main asymptotic parameters; we write $A\asymp B$ if $A\lesssim B$ and $B\lesssim A$.
If the implied constant is allowed to depend on auxiliary data $X$, we indicate this
by $A\lesssim_{X} B$ or $A=O_{X}(B)$.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Standing dependence convention.}
Unless explicitly indicated otherwise, implied constants may depend only on:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item the group $\Gamma$ (equivalently, the isometry class of $M$),
  \item the chosen cusp data (scaling matrices and widths, for the finitely many cusps),
  \item a fixed spectral-gap/exceptional-spectrum parameter (when invoked; see below),
  \item and finitely many auxiliary cutoffs fixed once and for all at their first appearance.
\end{itemize}
In particular, no estimate is permitted to conceal dependence on localization parameters
(e.g.\ $(\lambda,\eta)$) or on truncation heights $Y$; whenever such dependence is present,
it is displayed in the subscript of the corresponding $O(\cdot)$--notation.

\section{Geometric and spectral framework (summary)}\label{sec:prelim-framework}

We briefly summarize the conventions fixed in the blocks.

\subsection{Hyperbolic model and quotient}\label{subsec:prelim-hyp}

We work on the upper half-plane
\[
  \mathbb H=\{z=x+iy\in\mathbb C:\ y>0\},
  \qquad
  ds^{2}=\frac{dx^{2}+dy^{2}}{y^{2}},
  \qquad
  d\mu(z)=\frac{dx\,dy}{y^{2}}.
\]
Let $\Gamma\subset\PSL_{2}(\mathbb R)$ be a cofinite Fuchsian group and set
\[
  M=\Gamma\backslash \mathbb H,
  \qquad
  \vol(M)=\int_{M} d\mu <\infty.
\]
Cusps are allowed, and elliptic elements (orbifold points) are allowed unless a
statement explicitly restricts to the torsion-free case.

\subsection{Laplacian convention and spectral parameter}\label{subsec:prelim-laplacian}

The Laplace--Beltrami operator on $\mathbb H$ is taken as
\[
  \Delta = -y^{2}\big(\partial_{x}^{2}+\partial_{y}^{2}\big),
\]
so that $\Delta\ge 0$ on $M$.  Discrete eigenvalues are parameterized by
\[
  \Delta \varphi_j = \Big(\tfrac14+r_j^{2}\Big)\varphi_j,
  \qquad
  r_j\in\mathbb R_{\ge 0}\ \text{or}\ r_j\in i\,[0,\tfrac12],
\]
where $\{\varphi_j\}$ denotes an orthonormal basis of the discrete spectrum
(including the constant eigenfunction when present).

\subsection{Cusps, scaling matrices, and truncations}\label{subsec:prelim-cusps}

Let $\{\mathfrak a\}_{\mathfrak a=1}^{h}$ be the (finite) set of cusps.
For each cusp $\mathfrak a$ we fix a scaling matrix $\sigma_{\mathfrak a}\in\PSL_{2}(\mathbb R)$
with $\sigma_{\mathfrak a}(\infty)=\mathfrak a$, and a cusp width $w_{\mathfrak a}>0$ such that
\[
  \sigma_{\mathfrak a}^{-1}\Gamma_{\mathfrak a}\sigma_{\mathfrak a}
  =
  \left\{\pm
  \begin{pmatrix}1 & n w_{\mathfrak a}\\ 0 & 1\end{pmatrix}:\ n\in\mathbb Z\right\},
\]
where $\Gamma_{\mathfrak a}$ is the stabilizer of $\mathfrak a$ in $\Gamma$.
For $Y\ge 1$ we define the truncated surface $M(Y)$ by removing standard cusp neighborhoods
of height $\ge Y$ in the coordinates determined by the $\sigma_{\mathfrak a}$ (see
Block \texttt{02b-cusps} for the precise definition and disjointness threshold $Y_{0}$).
In these conventions one has, for all $Y\ge Y_{0}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:cusp-tail-volume}
  \vol\!\big(M\setminus M(Y)\big)=\sum_{\mathfrak a}\frac{w_{\mathfrak a}}{Y},
\end{equation}
and similarly
\[
  \length\big(\partial M(Y)\big)=\sum_{\mathfrak a}\frac{w_{\mathfrak a}}{Y}.
\]
These identities serve as reference formulas in truncation and Maass--Selberg arguments.

\subsection{Eisenstein series and scattering normalization}\label{subsec:prelim-eisenstein}

For each cusp $\mathfrak a$ and $\Re(s)>1$ we define the Eisenstein series
\[
  E_{\mathfrak a}(z,s)
  =
  \sum_{\gamma\in\Gamma_{\mathfrak a}\backslash\Gamma}
  \Im\big(\sigma_{\mathfrak a}^{-1}\gamma z\big)^{s},
\]
which admits meromorphic continuation to $s\in\mathbb C$.  The continuous spectrum
is parameterized by $s=\tfrac12+ir$, $r\in\mathbb R$, and the corresponding Plancherel
measure is
\[
  \frac{dr}{4\pi},
\]
with the inner-product normalization and Maass--Selberg relations fixed in
Block \texttt{02b-cusps}.  The scattering matrix $\Phi(s)$ is normalized in the
Hejhal convention; we write $\varphi_{\mathfrak a\mathfrak b}(s)$ for its entries.

\subsection{Exceptional spectrum parameter (when needed)}\label{subsec:prelim-exceptional}

If $M$ has cusps, discrete eigenvalues below $1/4$ may occur.  When uniform estimates
are sensitive to this phenomenon we allow dependence on a parameter $\beta\in[0,1/4]$
such that
\[
  \lambda_j\in(0,\tfrac14)
  \ \Longrightarrow\
  \lambda_j\le \tfrac14-\beta.
\]
Estimates in which this dependence is relevant are written with subscript
$O_{\Gamma,\beta}(\cdot)$ (or $\lesssim_{\Gamma,\beta}$).

\subsection{Selberg/Harish--Chandra transform conventions}\label{subsec:prelim-selberg}

Transform conventions for radial kernels on $\mathbb H$ (inversion, Plancherel,
and the Paley--Wiener correspondence) are fixed in Block \texttt{02b-selberg-transform}.
All subsequent trace identities and localized projectors use these conventions.
