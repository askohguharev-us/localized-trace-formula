\section*{Orientation and Intent}

This chapter freezes, once and for all, the analytic and geometric conventions
used throughout the monograph.  It is \emph{binding}: later kernel constructions,
projector identities, truncation commutators, and trace formula expansions cite
this chapter rather than reintroducing normalizations.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{What is frozen (and where).}
We fix:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item the hyperbolic model, metric, measure, distance, and Laplacian sign convention
  (Block \texttt{02b-geometry}, Sections~\ref{subsec:geometry-diamond} and references therein);
  \item cusp normalizations (scaling matrices, widths), truncation domains $M(Y)$,
  and canonical tail identities (Block \texttt{02b-cusps});
  \item Eisenstein series/scattering normalizations and the continuous Plancherel measure
  for $L^{2}(M)$ (Block \texttt{02b-cusps});
  \item Selberg/Harish--Chandra (spherical) transform conventions for radial kernels,
  including inversion/Plancherel/Paley--Wiener language (Block \texttt{02b-selberg-transform},
  Section~\ref{sec:selberg-transform}).
\end{itemize}

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Reproducibility principle (contract rule).}
Every implied constant in later $O(\cdot)$--bounds must have an admissible dependency
traceable to the dependency ledger declared below.  No estimate is allowed to hide
dependence on localization parameters $(\lambda,\eta)$ or truncation heights $Y$ unless
that dependence is explicitly annotated in the statement where it is used.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Primary references.}
Global conventions are compatible with classical sources, including
Selberg~\cite{Selberg1956}, Hejhal~\cite{Hejhal1983}, Buser~\cite{Buser1992},
and Iwaniec~\cite{Iwaniec2002}.  When a later statement depends on a specific
normalization choice, that dependence is traceable to this chapter.

\section*{Chapter Goals}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item[\textbf{(G1)}] Fix the geometric background:
  the upper half-plane $\mathbb{H}$ with hyperbolic metric and measure,
  the quotient $M=\Gamma\backslash\mathbb{H}$ for cofinite
  $\Gamma\subset\PSL_2(\mathbb{R})$, and the admissible geometric ledger.

  \item[\textbf{(G2)}] Fix the Laplacian convention and spectral parameterization:
  $\Delta\ge 0$, eigenvalues $\lambda=\tfrac14+r^2$, and the wave/heat/resolvent
  kernels used later as model operators and comparison kernels.

  \item[\textbf{(G3)}] Fix cusp neighborhoods, scaling matrices, cusp widths,
  and truncations $M(Y)$, including canonical tail volume/boundary identities.

  \item[\textbf{(G4)}] Fix the $L^2(M)$ spectral resolution conventions:
  discrete spectrum, Eisenstein series for the continuous spectrum,
  and the scattering normalization (including the continuous Plancherel measure).

  \item[\textbf{(G5)}] Fix Selberg/Harish--Chandra transform conventions
  for radial kernels: inversion, Plancherel, Paley--Wiener dictionary,
  and the admissible test-function class for trace identities.

  \item[\textbf{(G6)}] Fix Sobolev/elliptic conventions and the precise
  dependency rule for Sobolev constants on $M$ and on $M(Y)$.

  \item[\textbf{(G7)}] Enforce a strict dependency ledger:
  later implied constants depend only on ledger items below unless a statement
  explicitly enlarges the parameter set.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Invariants and Dependency Ledger}\label{sec:dependency-ledger}

We now record the \emph{persistent invariants} and admissible dependencies
of constants throughout the monograph.  This ledger is binding:
if a later argument requires dependence outside the items below,
it must explicitly declare an enlarged parameter set.

\subsection*{Geometric invariants}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]

  \item[\textbf{(I1)}] \textit{Hyperbolic model.}
  We work on the upper half-plane
  \[
    \mathbb{H}=\{z=x+iy\in\mathbb{C}:\ y>0\},
    \qquad
    ds^2=\frac{dx^2+dy^2}{y^2},
    \qquad
    d\mu(z)=\frac{dx\,dy}{y^2}.
  \]
  The hyperbolic distance is denoted $d_{\mathbb{H}}(z,w)$ and is normalized
  by the identity recorded in Block \texttt{02b-geometry}
  (see \eqref{eq:cosh-distance} therein).

  \item[\textbf{(I2)}] \textit{Quotient surface (finite area).}
  Let $\Gamma\subset\PSL_2(\mathbb{R})$ be a cofinite Fuchsian group and set
  \[
    M=\Gamma\backslash\mathbb{H},
    \qquad
    \vol(M)=\int_M d\mu <\infty.
  \]
  We allow cusps and (if present) elliptic elements (orbifold points),
  unless stated otherwise in a local argument.

  \item[\textbf{(I3)}] \textit{Cusp ledger.}
  Let $\{\mathfrak{a}\}_{\mathfrak{a}=1}^{h}$ be the cusps ($h\ge 0$).
  For each cusp $\mathfrak{a}$ we fix:
  \begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
    \item a scaling matrix $\sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}\in\PSL_2(\mathbb{R})$ with
          $\sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}(\infty)=\mathfrak{a}$;
    \item a width $w_{\mathfrak{a}}>0$ satisfying
    \[
      \sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}^{-1}\Gamma_{\mathfrak{a}}\sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}
      = \left\{\pm\begin{pmatrix}1 & n w_{\mathfrak{a}}\\ 0 & 1\end{pmatrix}: n\in\mathbb{Z}\right\},
    \]
    where $\Gamma_{\mathfrak{a}}$ is the stabilizer of $\mathfrak{a}$ in $\Gamma$.
  \end{itemize}
  Cusp-dependent constants may depend on the finite collection
  $\big(\{w_{\mathfrak{a}}\},\{\sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}\}\big)$ and on $\Gamma$,
  but never on truncation heights $Y$ unless explicitly annotated.

  \item[\textbf{(I4)}] \textit{Truncations and canonical tail identity.}
  For $Y\ge 1$, define the truncated surface
  \[
    M(Y):=M\setminus \bigcup_{\mathfrak{a}}
    \sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}\Big(\{x+iy:\ y\ge Y\}/(x\sim x+w_{\mathfrak{a}})\Big),
  \]
  with a fixed choice of $Y_0=Y_0(\Gamma)$ ensuring these cusp tails are disjoint
  for all $Y\ge Y_0$ (fixed once and for all in Block \texttt{02b-cusps}).
  In these conventions one has the exact identity
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:cusp-tail-volume}
    \vol\!\big(M\setminus M(Y)\big)=\sum_{\mathfrak{a}}\frac{w_{\mathfrak{a}}}{Y},
    \qquad (Y\ge Y_0),
  \end{equation}
  and the boundary length identity
  \[
    \operatorname{length}\big(\partial M(Y)\big)=\sum_{\mathfrak{a}}\frac{w_{\mathfrak{a}}}{Y}.
  \]
  These formulas are canonical references for truncation and Maass--Selberg estimates.

\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Spectral invariants}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]

  \item[\textbf{(I5)}] \textit{Laplacian convention and discrete spectrum.}
  The Laplace--Beltrami operator $\Delta$ on $M$ is nonnegative and on $\mathbb H$
  is given in coordinates by
  \[
    \Delta = -y^{2}\big(\partial_{x}^{2}+\partial_{y}^{2}\big).
  \]
  Discrete eigenvalues are parameterized as
  \[
    \Delta \varphi_j = \Big(\tfrac14+r_j^2\Big)\varphi_j,
    \qquad
    r_j\in\mathbb{R}_{\ge 0}\ \text{or}\ r_j\in i[0,\tfrac12],
  \]
  where $\{\varphi_j\}$ is an orthonormal basis for the discrete spectrum
  (including the constant eigenfunction when $\vol(M)<\infty$).

  \item[\textbf{(I6)}] \textit{Eisenstein series (cusp normalization).}
  For each cusp $\mathfrak{a}$ define, for $\Re(s)>1$,
  \[
    E_{\mathfrak{a}}(z,s)=
    \sum_{\gamma\in\Gamma_{\mathfrak{a}}\backslash\Gamma}
    \Im\big(\sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}^{-1}\gamma z\big)^{s},
  \]
  which continues meromorphically to $s\in\mathbb{C}$ and is holomorphic on
  $\Re(s)=1/2$ away from possible residual poles (treated explicitly in the cusp block).
  The continuous spectrum is parameterized by $s=1/2+ir$, $r\in\mathbb{R}$.

  \item[\textbf{(I7)}] \textit{Scattering matrix and continuous Plancherel measure.}
  Let $\Phi(s)$ denote the $h\times h$ scattering matrix in the Hejhal convention,
  with entries $\varphi_{\mathfrak{a}\mathfrak{b}}(s)$.
  The continuous part of the $L^2(M)$ spectral resolution uses the standard
  Plancherel measure
  \[
    \frac{dr}{4\pi}
  \]
  on $r\in\mathbb{R}$, together with the (block-recorded) normalization of
  the Eisenstein inner products and Maass--Selberg relations.

  \item[\textbf{(I8)}] \textit{Exceptional spectrum bookkeeping (optional parameter).}
  If $M$ has cusps, there may exist discrete eigenvalues $\lambda_j<1/4$.
  When an argument requires uniform control that is sensitive to this phenomenon,
  we allow it to depend on an explicit parameter $\beta\in[0,1/4]$ such that
  \[
    \lambda_j\in(0,\tfrac14)\ \Longrightarrow\ \lambda_j\le \tfrac14-\beta.
  \]
  Any estimate using scattering bounds that depend on exceptional spectrum is
  annotated as $O_{\Gamma,\beta}(\cdot)$.

\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Propagation scale and semiclassical bookkeeping}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item[\textbf{(I9)}] \textit{Semiclassical parameter.}
  In high-frequency regimes we write $h=\lambda^{-1}$ (or $h=r^{-1}$ when
  working directly with the spectral parameter $r$).  Every semiclassical
  statement specifies which parameter is used.

  \item[\textbf{(I10)}] \textit{Logarithmic (Ehrenfest-type) time scale.}
  In microlocal propagation arguments the natural maximal time is
  \[
    T \asymp \log \lambda,
  \]
  reflecting exponential area growth and instability of the geodesic flow.
  Precise constants (and the regime in which they are admissible) are fixed
  in Chapter~5; until then, \eqref{eq:ball-area} in Block \texttt{02b-geometry}
  is the sole geometric input used to motivate this scale.

\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Asymptotic notation and admissible dependencies}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item[\textbf{(I11)}] \textit{Asymptotic notation.}
  We write $A\lesssim B$ if $A\le C B$ for an absolute constant $C>0$;
  $A\asymp B$ if $A\lesssim B$ and $B\lesssim A$.
  We write $O_X(\cdot)$ to indicate that the implied constant may depend only
  on parameters in the set $X$.

  \item[\textbf{(I12)}] \textit{Strict ledger rule (no hidden dependencies).}
  Unless explicitly stated otherwise, any implied constant in this monograph
  may depend only on:
  \[
    \Gamma,\quad \{w_{\mathfrak{a}}\},\quad \{\sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}\},\quad \beta,
    \quad \text{and fixed auxiliary cutoffs chosen once and for all.}
  \]
  In particular, no constant may depend on localization window parameters
  $(\lambda,\eta)$ or truncation heights $Y$ unless that dependence is
  explicitly annotated.

\end{itemize}

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Audit note (enforcement).}
Ledger items (I1)--(I12) are the only admissible sources of dependence for
constants in later estimates.  This rule is enforced in chapter audits
and in the statements of main theorems: any deviation must be declared.

\section*{Organization of the Chapter}

The chapter is split into three technical blocks, each intended to be cited as a
standalone reference module.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item \textbf{Geometry (Block \texttt{02b-geometry}).}
  Metric/distance/geodesics, isometries and translation length, exponential
  volume growth, thick--thin/collars, Laplacian convention, spherical functions
  and Plancherel density on $\mathbb H$, and model kernels (heat/wave/resolvent)
  together with safe Poincar\'e summation criteria.

  \item \textbf{Cusps and truncation (Block \texttt{02b-cusps}).}
  Scaling matrices, cusp widths, truncation domains $M(Y)$, sharp and smoothed
  truncations, Fourier expansions at cusps, Maass--Selberg relations, Eisenstein
  series conventions, and scattering normalization.

  \item \textbf{Selberg transform (Block \texttt{02b-selberg-transform}).}
  Selberg/Harish--Chandra transform conventions for radial kernels, inversion,
  Plancherel and Paley--Wiener duality, and canonical examples used later for
  localized projectors and trace identities.
\end{itemize}

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Reader guidance.}
Later chapters cite these blocks as immutable convention sources:
any formula in Chapters~3--8 involving a radial kernel, cusp truncation,
or Eisenstein/scattering term uses the normalization fixed here.

\section*{Chapter Audit}

\noindent\textbf{(A2.1) Geometry and measure fixed.}
The hyperbolic model $(\mathbb{H},ds^2,d\mu)$, the quotient $M=\Gamma\backslash\mathbb{H}$,
and the admissible geometric ledger are fixed and immutable (Block \texttt{02b-geometry}).
No later argument redefines metric/measure/distance.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(A2.2) Cusps and truncation fixed.}
For each cusp $\mathfrak{a}$ we fixed a scaling matrix $\sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}$
and width $w_{\mathfrak{a}}$, and defined truncations $M(Y)$ by height cutoffs.
The canonical tail volume identity \eqref{eq:cusp-tail-volume} and boundary length
identity are recorded as references for truncation and Maass--Selberg computations
(Block \texttt{02b-cusps}).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(A2.3) Spectral conventions fixed.}
We fixed the nonnegative Laplacian convention, the parameterization
$\lambda=\tfrac14+r^2$, and Eisenstein/scattering normalization, including the
continuous Plancherel measure $dr/(4\pi)$ (Block \texttt{02b-cusps}).
All later spectral expansions and trace identities use these normalizations.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(A2.4) Exceptional spectrum bookkeeping declared.}
When required, dependence on exceptional spectrum is mediated by an explicit
parameter $\beta$ and is displayed as $O_{\Gamma,\beta}(\cdot)$.
No estimate is allowed to hide gap/exceptional-spectrum dependence.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(A2.5) Selberg transform conventions fixed.}
The Selberg/Harish--Chandra transform is delegated to Block \texttt{02b-selberg-transform}
and treated as immutable for the rest of the monograph.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(A2.6) Dependency ledger enforced.}
The strict dependency ledger (I1)--(I12) is binding.
Every later implied constant is traceable to these invariants (and fixed auxiliary cutoffs),
with no hidden dependence on window parameters $(\lambda,\eta)$ or truncation heights $Y$
unless explicitly annotated.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Conclusion.}
All goals (G1)--(G7) are achieved.  This chapter is certified as the unique
normalization reference point for the analytic developments in Chapters~3--8.
