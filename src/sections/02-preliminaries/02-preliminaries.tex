\section*{Orientation and Intent}

This chapter fixes, once and for all, the analytic and geometric conventions
used throughout the monograph.  Its purpose is foundational: every later
kernel construction, projector identity, and trace formula statement will
cite this chapter rather than reintroduce conventions.  In particular, we
freeze:
(i) the hyperbolic model, metric, and measure;
(ii) the Laplace--Beltrami operator and spectral parameter conventions;
(iii) cusp normalizations, scaling matrices, and truncation domains;
(iv) Eisenstein series and scattering normalization, including Plancherel
measure for the continuous spectrum;
(v) the Selberg/Harish--Chandra transform conventions for radial kernels;
(vi) the dependency rules for all implied constants.

The guiding principle is \emph{reproducibility}.  Every constant occurring in
later $O(\cdot)$--estimates must have an explicit admissible dependency on
geometric and spectral invariants recorded below.  No later bound may hide a
dependence on window parameters $(\lambda,\eta)$ or truncation parameters
unless explicitly stated.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Primary references.}
Our global conventions are compatible with classical sources, including
Selberg~\cite{Selberg1956}, Hejhal~\cite{Hejhal1983}, Buser~\cite{Buser1992},
and Iwaniec~\cite{Iwaniec2002}.  Whenever a statement later depends on
a specific normalization choice, that dependence is traceable to this chapter.

\section*{Chapter Goals}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item[\textbf{(G1)}] Fix the geometric background:
  the upper half-plane $\mathbb{H}$ with its hyperbolic metric,
  the quotient $M=\Gamma\backslash\mathbb{H}$ for a cofinite
  $\Gamma\subset\PSL_2(\mathbb{R})$, and the associated measure and volume.

  \item[\textbf{(G2)}] Fix the Laplacian convention:
  $\Delta\ge 0$, spectral parameter $\lambda=\tfrac14+r^2$ with $r\ge 0$,
  and the wave group $U(t)=e^{it\sqrt{\Delta-1/4}}$ used later in projector
  constructions.

  \item[\textbf{(G3)}] Fix cusp neighborhoods, scaling matrices, cusp widths,
  and truncations $M(Y)$ with explicit volume/boundary formulas.

  \item[\textbf{(G4)}] Fix the spectral decomposition of $L^2(M)$ into the
  discrete spectrum and Eisenstein series, together with the continuous
  Plancherel measure $dr/(4\pi)$ and the scattering normalization.

  \item[\textbf{(G5)}] Fix the Selberg/Harish--Chandra transform conventions
  for radial kernels and the corresponding Plancherel/Paley--Wiener framework.

  \item[\textbf{(G6)}] Declare the Sobolev scales and mapping conventions that
  later control stationary phase, truncation commutators, and error terms.

  \item[\textbf{(G7)}] Declare a strict dependency ledger: all implied
  constants may depend only on the invariants recorded below unless
  explicitly annotated otherwise.
\end{itemize}

\section*{Invariants and Dependency Ledger}\label{sec:dependency-ledger}

We now record the \emph{persistent invariants} and the admissible dependencies
of constants throughout the monograph.  This ledger is binding: if a later
argument requires dependence outside the items below, it must explicitly
declare an enlarged parameter set.

\subsection*{Geometric invariants}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item[\textbf{(I1)}] \textit{Hyperbolic model.}
  We work on the upper half-plane
  \[
    \mathbb{H}=\{z=x+iy\in\mathbb{C}:\ y>0\},
    \qquad
    ds^2=\frac{dx^2+dy^2}{y^2},
    \qquad
    d\mu(z)=\frac{dx\,dy}{y^2}.
  \]
  The hyperbolic distance is denoted $d_{\mathbb{H}}(z,w)$.

  \item[\textbf{(I2)}] \textit{Quotient surface.}
  Let $\Gamma\subset\PSL_2(\mathbb{R})$ be a cofinite Fuchsian group and set
  \[
    M=\Gamma\backslash\mathbb{H},
    \qquad
    \vol(M)=\int_M d\mu <\infty.
  \]
  We allow cusps and (if present) elliptic elements, unless explicitly stated
  otherwise in later chapters.

  \item[\textbf{(I3)}] \textit{Cusp data.}
  Let $\{\mathfrak{a}\}_{\mathfrak{a}=1}^{h}$ denote the set of cusps ($h\ge 0$).
  For each cusp $\mathfrak{a}$ fix:
  \begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
    \item a scaling matrix $\sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}\in\PSL_2(\mathbb{R})$ such that
    $\sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}(\infty)=\mathfrak{a}$;
    \item a width $w_{\mathfrak{a}}>0$ such that
    \[
      \sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}^{-1}\Gamma_{\mathfrak{a}}\sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}
      = \left\{\pm\begin{pmatrix}1 & n w_{\mathfrak{a}}\\ 0 & 1\end{pmatrix}: n\in\mathbb{Z}\right\},
    \]
    where $\Gamma_{\mathfrak{a}}$ is the stabilizer of $\mathfrak{a}$ in $\Gamma$.
  \end{itemize}
  All cusp-dependent constants may depend on the finite collection
  $\big(\{w_{\mathfrak{a}}\},\{\sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}\}\big)$ and on $\Gamma$,
  but never on truncation heights $Y$ unless explicitly annotated.

  \item[\textbf{(I4)}] \textit{Truncation domains.}
  For $Y\ge 1$, define the truncated surface
  \[
    M(Y):=M\setminus \bigcup_{\mathfrak{a}}
    \sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}\Big(\{x+iy:\ y\ge Y\}/(x\sim x+w_{\mathfrak{a}})\Big),
  \]
  where the cusp neighborhoods are taken disjoint for $Y$ sufficiently large
  (a choice fixed once and for all).  In these conventions the cusp tail volume
  satisfies the identity
  \begin{equation}\label{eq:cusp-tail-volume}
    \vol\!\big(M\setminus M(Y)\big)=\sum_{\mathfrak{a}}\frac{w_{\mathfrak{a}}}{Y}.
  \end{equation}
  The boundary length of $\partial M(Y)$ equals $\sum_{\mathfrak{a}} w_{\mathfrak{a}}/Y$.
  These formulas are used repeatedly in truncation and Maass--Selberg estimates.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Spectral invariants}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item[\textbf{(I5)}] \textit{Laplacian convention.}
  The Laplace--Beltrami operator $\Delta$ on $M$ is nonnegative:
  in local coordinates it is given by
  \[
    \Delta = -y^{2}\big(\partial_{x}^{2}+\partial_{y}^{2}\big).
  \]
  Discrete eigenvalues are parameterized as
  \[
    \Delta \varphi_j = \Big(\tfrac14+r_j^2\Big)\varphi_j,
    \qquad
    r_j\in\mathbb{R}_{\ge 0},
  \]
  where $\{\varphi_j\}$ is an orthonormal basis of $L^2(M)$ for the discrete
  spectrum (including the constant eigenfunction when $M$ has finite volume).

  \item[\textbf{(I6)}] \textit{Eisenstein series normalization.}
  For each cusp $\mathfrak{a}$ define the Eisenstein series for $\Re(s)>1$ by
  \[
    E_{\mathfrak{a}}(z,s)=
    \sum_{\gamma\in\Gamma_{\mathfrak{a}}\backslash\Gamma}
    \Im\big(\sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}^{-1}\gamma z\big)^{s},
  \]
  continued meromorphically to $s\in\mathbb{C}$ and holomorphic on $\Re(s)=1/2$
  except possibly at $s=1/2$ for residual spectrum.  The continuous spectrum is
  parameterized by $s=1/2+ir$, $r\in\mathbb{R}$.

  \item[\textbf{(I7)}] \textit{Scattering normalization and Plancherel measure.}
  Let $\Phi(s)$ denote the $h\times h$ scattering matrix in the Hejhal convention,
  and write $\varphi_{\mathfrak{a}\mathfrak{b}}(s)$ for its entries.  We adopt
  the standard Plancherel measure
  \[
    \frac{dr}{4\pi}
  \]
  for the continuous spectrum in the spectral resolution of $L^2(M)$.

  \item[\textbf{(I8)}] \textit{Spectral gap parameter.}
  If $M$ has cusps then there may exist discrete eigenvalues $\lambda_j<1/4$.
  We fix a parameter $\beta\in[0,1/4]$ such that
  \[
    \text{every nonzero discrete eigenvalue }\lambda_j
    \text{ satisfies either }\lambda_j\ge \tfrac14
    \text{ or }\lambda_j\in[0,\tfrac14-\beta].
  \]
  Equivalently, the spectral parameters satisfy $r_j\in\mathbb{R}\cup i[0,1/2]$
  and the exceptional imaginary parameters obey
  \(
    \Im(r_j)\le \tfrac12-\sqrt{\beta}.
  \)
  Any estimate that uses cusp scattering bounds may depend on $\beta$; this
  dependence is always displayed as $O_{\Gamma,\beta}(\cdot)$.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Propagation scale and semiclassical bookkeeping}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item[\textbf{(I9)}] \textit{Semiclassical parameter.}
  In high-frequency regimes we write $h=\lambda^{-1}$ (or $h=r^{-1}$ when
  working directly with $r$).  All semiclassical statements specify which
  parameter is used.

  \item[\textbf{(I10)}] \textit{Ehrenfest time scale.}
  In microlocal propagation arguments the natural maximal time is
  \[
    T \asymp \log \lambda,
  \]
  reflecting exponential volume growth and the instability of the geodesic
  flow.  The implicit constants in $\asymp$ are fixed once and for all in
  Chapter~5 and are treated as universal.
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Asymptotic notation and admissible dependencies}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item[\textbf{(I11)}] \textit{Asymptotic notation.}
  We write $A\lesssim B$ if $A\le C B$ for an absolute constant $C>0$;
  $A\asymp B$ if both $A\lesssim B$ and $B\lesssim A$.
  We write $O_X(\cdot)$ to indicate that the implied constant may depend only
  on parameters in the set $X$.

  \item[\textbf{(I12)}] \textit{Strict ledger rule.}
  Unless explicitly stated otherwise, any implied constant in this monograph
  may depend only on:
  \[
    \Gamma,\quad \{w_{\mathfrak{a}}\},\quad \{\sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}\},\quad \beta,
    \quad \text{and fixed auxiliary cutoffs chosen once and for all.}
  \]
  In particular, no constant may depend on localization window parameters
  $(\lambda,\eta)$ or truncation heights $Y$ unless such dependence is
  explicitly annotated.
\end{itemize}

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Audit note.}
The ledger items (I1)--(I12) are the only admissible sources of dependence for
constants in later estimates.  This rule is enforced in the chapter audits
and in the statements of main theorems.

\section*{Organization of the Chapter}

The chapter is split into three technical blocks, each intended to be cited as
a standalone reference module.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em]
  \item \textbf{Geometry (Block 02b-geometry).}
  Metric, distance, geodesics, volume growth, injectivity considerations,
  and standard kernels (heat/wave/resolvent) on $\mathbb{H}$ and their descent
  to $M$.

  \item \textbf{Cusps and truncation (Block 02b-cusps).}
  Scaling matrices, cusp widths, truncation domains $M(Y)$, sharp and smoothed
  truncation operators, Fourier expansions at cusps, Maass--Selberg relations,
  and scattering conventions.

  \item \textbf{Selberg transform (Block 02b-selberg-transform).}
  Selberg/Harish--Chandra transform conventions for radial kernels, inversion,
  Plancherel/Paley--Wiener statements, and canonical examples used later for
  localized projectors and trace identities.
\end{itemize}

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{Reader guidance.}
Later chapters cite these blocks as immutable convention sources: if a formula
in Chapters~3--8 involves a radial kernel or a cusp truncation, the
normalizations are those fixed here.

\section*{Chapter Audit}

\noindent\textbf{(A2.1) Geometry and measure fixed.}
We fixed the hyperbolic model $(\mathbb{H},ds^2)$, the quotient
$M=\Gamma\backslash\mathbb{H}$, and the hyperbolic measure $d\mu$.
The volume $\vol(M)$ is finite and appears as the unique global geometric
coefficient in Weyl-type main terms.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(A2.2) Cusps and truncation fixed.}
For each cusp $\mathfrak{a}$ we fixed a scaling matrix $\sigma_{\mathfrak{a}}$
and a width $w_{\mathfrak{a}}$, and defined truncations $M(Y)$ by height cutoffs.
The tail volume identity~\eqref{eq:cusp-tail-volume} and the matching boundary
length formula are recorded as canonical references for later truncation and
Maass--Selberg computations.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(A2.3) Spectral conventions fixed.}
We fixed the nonnegative Laplacian convention, the parameterization
$\lambda=\tfrac14+r^2$, and the Eisenstein normalization, including the
continuous Plancherel measure $dr/(4\pi)$ in the Hejhal convention.
All later spectral expansions and trace identities use these normalizations.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(A2.4) Spectral gap parameter declared.}
We recorded a gap parameter $\beta$ as an admissible input for cusp/scattering
bounds and required that all such dependencies be displayed as
$O_{\Gamma,\beta}(\cdot)$.  No later estimate is allowed to hide spectral-gap
dependence.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(A2.5) Selberg transform conventions fixed.}
The Selberg/Harish--Chandra transform conventions are delegated to
\texttt{02b-selberg-transform.tex} and are treated as immutable for the rest
of the monograph; in particular, all later localized test functions inherit
their normalization from that block.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{(A2.6) Dependency ledger enforced.}
We declared the strict dependency ledger (I1)--(I12).  Every later implied
constant must be traceable to those invariants (and fixed auxiliary cutoffs),
with no hidden dependence on window parameters $(\lambda,\eta)$ or truncation
heights $Y$ unless explicitly stated.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Conclusion.}
All goals (G1)--(G7) are achieved.  The chapter is certified as the unique
normalization reference point for the analytic developments in
Chapters~3--8.
