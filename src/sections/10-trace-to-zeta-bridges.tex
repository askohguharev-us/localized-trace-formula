\chapter{Trace-to-zeta bridges: determinants, explicit formulae, and positivity criteria}
\label{chap:10-trace-to-zeta}

\section{Overview}
\label{sec:10.overview}

The purpose of this chapter is to record, in a form compatible with the normalized trace framework developed in
Chapter~\ref{chap:08-normalization}, the standard analytic bridges between trace formulae and zeta-type objects.
These bridges are of three complementary types:
(i) passage from trace identities to regularized determinants,
(ii) derivation of explicit formulae relating spectral parameters to geometric/arithmetic data,
and (iii) construction of positivity criteria (quadratic forms) whose nonnegativity constrains the location of
spectral parameters.

The emphasis throughout is on formulations that are stable under admissible auxiliary choices and that isolate the
identity and continuous-spectrum contributions explicitly.

\section{Regularized determinants from trace data}
\label{sec:10.det}

\subsection{Spectral zeta functions and zeta-regularized determinants}
\label{subsec:10.det.zeta}

Let $A$ be a self-adjoint operator acting on a separable Hilbert space $H$, with spectrum $\Spec(A)$.
Assume that $A$ is bounded below and that its resolvent is compact, so that the spectrum is discrete with finite
multiplicity and accumulates only at $+\infty$.
Let $\{\lambda_j\}_{j\ge 1}$ denote the nonzero eigenvalues of $A$, repeated with multiplicity.

For $\Re(s)$ sufficiently large one defines the spectral zeta function
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.zetaA.def}
\zeta_A(s)
:=\sum_{j\ge 1}\lambda_j^{-s}.
\end{equation}
If $\zeta_A(s)$ admits a meromorphic continuation to a neighborhood of $s=0$ which is holomorphic at $s=0$, one
defines the zeta-regularized determinant by
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.detA.def}
\det\nolimits_{\zeta}(A)
:=\exp\!\bigl(-\zeta_A'(0)\bigr).
\end{equation}

A standard route to \eqref{eq:10.zetaA.def} is through heat traces.
If $e^{-tA}$ is trace class for all $t>0$ and if $\Tr(e^{-tA})$ admits an asymptotic expansion as $t\downarrow 0$,
then for $\Re(s)$ sufficiently large one has the Mellin representation
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.mellin}
\zeta_A(s)
=
\frac{1}{\Gamma(s)}
\int_0^{\infty} t^{s-1}\bigl(\Tr(e^{-tA})-\Pi_0\bigr)\,dt,
\end{equation}
where $\Pi_0$ denotes the contribution of the nullspace of $A$.
Analytic continuation of \eqref{eq:10.mellin} yields the meromorphic continuation of $\zeta_A$.

\subsection{Trace formula input}
\label{subsec:10.det.trace-input}

In the geometric settings of interest, the trace formula yields distributional identities of the form
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.trace.distributional}
\Tr\bigl(\varphi(A)\bigr)
=
\mathcal{I}[\varphi]
+\mathcal{G}[\varphi]
+\mathcal{C}[\varphi],
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{I}$ is the identity contribution, $\mathcal{G}$ is the geometric contribution
(hyperbolic/elliptic/orbital integrals, depending on the setting), and $\mathcal{C}$ is the continuous-spectrum
contribution (scattering terms).  The precise form of \eqref{eq:10.trace.distributional} depends on the underlying
space and on the operator $A$.

The normalization procedure of Chapter~\ref{chap:08-normalization} produces a canonical trace functional
$\mathcal{T}$ which removes the identity component in a controlled manner.  In particular, for admissible test
functions $h$ the functional $\mathcal{T}[h]$ is stable under auxiliary choices and isolates the remaining terms
of the trace formula.

For the purposes of this chapter, we treat $\mathcal{T}$ as the canonical trace input for the construction of
determinant-level quantities.

\section{Smoothed oscillatory traces}
\label{sec:10.osc}

\subsection{A standard test family}
\label{subsec:10.osc.family}

Let $W\in C_c^\infty(\R)$ be an even function.
For parameters $(\tau,\Delta)\in\R\times (0,\infty)$ define
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.htaudelta}
h_{\tau,\Delta}(t)
:=
W(t/\Delta)e^{-i\tau t}.
\end{equation}
The Fourier transform satisfies
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.hhat}
\widehat{h_{\tau,\Delta}}(\xi)
=
\Delta\,\widehat{W}\bigl(\Delta(\xi-\tau)\bigr),
\end{equation}
so that $h_{\tau,\Delta}$ localizes the spectral side near frequency $\xi=\tau$ with resolution $\asymp \Delta^{-1}$.

Define the smoothed oscillatory trace functional
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.Ddef}
\mathfrak{D}(\tau;\Delta)
:=
\mathcal{T}\!\left[h_{\tau,\Delta}\right].
\end{equation}

In the classical trace formula regime, $\mathfrak{D}(\tau;\Delta)$ can be interpreted as a regularized version of a
spectral density observable.  Its main virtue is that it admits a stable decomposition into geometric and spectral
contributions, with the identity term removed.

\subsection{Stability under admissible modifications}
\label{subsec:10.osc.stability}

The following statement is an immediate consequence of the stability results proved in
Chapter~\ref{chap:08-normalization}.

\begin{proposition}[Stability of $\mathfrak{D}(\tau;\Delta)$]
\label{prop:10.D.stability}
Let $\mathfrak{D}(\tau;\Delta)$ be defined by \eqref{eq:10.Ddef} using admissible auxiliary choices.
If $\mathfrak{D}'(\tau;\Delta)$ is defined using any other admissible choices, then for all $(\tau,\Delta)$ in the
validity regime one has
\[
\mathfrak{D}(\tau;\Delta)-\mathfrak{D}'(\tau;\Delta)
=
\Delta_{\mathrm{id}}(\tau,\Delta)
+
\Err(\tau,\Delta),
\]
where $\Delta_{\mathrm{id}}(\tau,\Delta)$ is an explicitly computable identity-term correction and
\[
|\Err(\tau,\Delta)|
\le
\mathcal{E}\!\left[h_{\tau,\Delta}\right]
+
\mathcal{E}'\!\left[h_{\tau,\Delta}\right],
\]
with $\mathcal{E}$ and $\mathcal{E}'$ denoting the corresponding error ledgers.
\end{proposition}

\section{Zeta-type functions as trace encoders}
\label{sec:10.zeta-encoders}

\subsection{Logarithmic derivatives and Euler products}
\label{subsec:10.euler}

In many trace formula settings, the geometric side is naturally encoded by an Euler product.
The archetypal example is the Selberg zeta function on compact hyperbolic surfaces, where the primitive closed
geodesics encode the hyperbolic conjugacy classes.

Let $\{\gamma\}$ range over primitive hyperbolic conjugacy classes and let $\ell(\gamma)$ denote their lengths.
Assume that there exists a zeta-type function $Z(s)$, holomorphic in a right half-plane, such that
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.logder}
-\frac{Z'(s)}{Z(s)}
=
\sum_{\{\gamma\}}
\sum_{m\ge 1}
a(\gamma^m)\,e^{-sm\ell(\gamma)},
\qquad
\Re(s)\gg 1,
\end{equation}
where $a(\gamma^m)$ are the standard amplitude factors arising in the geometric side of the trace formula.

Equation \eqref{eq:10.logder} expresses the geometric side as a Laplace transform of the length spectrum.
The analytic continuation of $Z(s)$ is then intimately linked to the analytic continuation of the trace formula.

\subsection{Spectral interpretation}
\label{subsec:10.spectral-interp}

In rank-one compact cases, the zeros of $Z(s)$ are known to encode the Laplace eigenvalues.
More generally, in noncompact settings, zeros and poles of scattering determinants contribute additional spectral
parameters.  In all such cases, the trace formula provides a distributional identity relating sums over spectral
parameters to sums over lengths.

In particular, after normalization, one obtains a canonical equality of the form
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.explicit.schematic}
\mathcal{T}[h]
=
\mathcal{G}[h]
+
\mathcal{S}[h]
+
\Err[h],
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{G}[h]$ is a geometric sum involving $\widehat{h}(\ell(\gamma))$, $\mathcal{S}[h]$ is a spectral sum
(or spectral integral) involving $\widehat{h}$ evaluated at the spectral parameters, and $\Err[h]$ is controlled by
the consolidated ledger.

\section{Explicit formulae in normalized form}
\label{sec:10.explicit}

\subsection{A general template}
\label{subsec:10.explicit.template}

Let $h$ be an admissible test function.  The trace formula in its normalized form yields an explicit formula
relating spectral and geometric data.  We record this in a stable formulation.

\begin{theorem}[Normalized explicit formula]
\label{thm:10.normalized-explicit}
Let $h$ be admissible.  Then
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.explicit}
\mathcal{T}[h]
=
\mathcal{G}[h]
+
\mathcal{S}[h]
+
\Err[h],
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{enumerate}
\item $\mathcal{G}[h]$ is the geometric contribution, expressible as a sum of the form
\[
\mathcal{G}[h]
=
\sum_{\{\gamma\}}
\sum_{m\ge 1}
a(\gamma^m)\,\widehat{h}(m\ell(\gamma))
\]
whenever the setting admits the decomposition \eqref{eq:10.logder};
\item $\mathcal{S}[h]$ is the spectral contribution, expressible as a sum (or integral) over spectral parameters
$\{\rho\}$ of the form
\[
\mathcal{S}[h]
=
\sum_{\rho}
b(\rho)\,\widehat{h}(\rho),
\]
with coefficients $b(\rho)$ determined by multiplicities or scattering weights;
\item the error term satisfies
\[
|\Err[h]|
\le
\mathcal{E}[h],
\]
where $\mathcal{E}[h]$ is the consolidated ledger.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
This is a direct consequence of the trace formula decomposition combined with the normalization procedure of
Chapter~\ref{chap:08-normalization}.  The bound on $\Err[h]$ follows from the definition of the consolidated ledger.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Smoothed explicit formula near a frequency}
\label{subsec:10.explicit.smoothed}

Applying Theorem~\ref{thm:10.normalized-explicit} to $h_{\tau,\Delta}$ from \eqref{eq:10.htaudelta}, one obtains
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.explicit.smoothed.eq}
\mathfrak{D}(\tau;\Delta)
=
\mathcal{G}[h_{\tau,\Delta}]
+
\mathcal{S}[h_{\tau,\Delta}]
+
\Err(\tau,\Delta),
\end{equation}
with $|\Err(\tau,\Delta)|\le \mathcal{E}[h_{\tau,\Delta}]$.

In many situations, $\mathcal{S}[h_{\tau,\Delta}]$ may be interpreted as a smoothed spectral density near $\tau$,
while $\mathcal{G}[h_{\tau,\Delta}]$ is a smoothed geometric observable involving $\widehat{W}$ evaluated at
$\Delta(\cdot-\tau)$.

\section{Quadratic forms and positivity criteria}
\label{sec:10.positivity}

\subsection{A canonical nonnegative test construction}
\label{subsec:10.pos.conv}

Let $h$ be an admissible test function.  Define the involution
\[
h^\ast(t):=\overline{h(-t)},
\]
and the convolution
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.convolution}
(h\star h^\ast)(t)
:=
\int_{\R} h(t-u)\,h^\ast(u)\,du.
\end{equation}
Then
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.convolution.fourier}
\widehat{h\star h^\ast}(\xi)=|\widehat{h}(\xi)|^2\ge 0.
\end{equation}

The construction \eqref{eq:10.convolution} is canonical and yields a nonnegative spectral weight.
Accordingly, applying the trace functional to $h\star h^\ast$ produces a quadratic form.

\begin{definition}[Positivity quadratic form]
\label{def:10.Qdef}
Define
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.Q}
\mathcal{Q}[h]
:=
\mathcal{T}[h\star h^\ast].
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

\subsection{Positivity modulo explicit correction}
\label{subsec:10.pos.gate}

The following theorem is the analytic core of the positivity bridge.

\begin{theorem}[Positivity gate]
\label{thm:10.pos-gate}
Let $h$ be admissible.  Then there exists a spectral quantity $\mathcal{P}[h]\ge 0$ such that
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.posgate}
\mathcal{Q}[h]
=
\mathcal{P}[h]
+
\mathcal{G}[h\star h^\ast]
+
\Err^{\mathrm{pos}}[h],
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{G}[h\star h^\ast]$ is the geometric contribution evaluated at $h\star h^\ast$ and
\[
|\Err^{\mathrm{pos}}[h]|
\le
\mathcal{E}[h\star h^\ast].
\]
In particular, whenever the geometric term is explicitly controlled and the ledger is negligible in the relevant
regime, the functional $\mathcal{Q}[h]$ is nonnegative up to an explicit, computable correction.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By \eqref{eq:10.convolution.fourier}, the spectral side of the trace formula evaluated at $h\star h^\ast$ integrates
the spectral measure against the nonnegative weight $|\widehat{h}|^2$, and hence produces a nonnegative quantity
$\mathcal{P}[h]$.  The remaining contributions are the geometric term and the controlled error term.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Consequences for spectral parameter localization}
\label{subsec:10.pos.localization}

In settings where the spectral parameters correspond to zeros of a zeta-type function, positivity criteria of the
form \eqref{eq:10.posgate} are the natural analytic mechanism underlying Li--Weil type conditions.
Indeed, if a family of admissible test functions $\{h\}$ can be chosen so that the geometric contribution is
explicitly bounded and the ledger is negligible, then the inequality
\[
\mathcal{Q}[h]\ge -\bigl|\mathcal{G}[h\star h^\ast]\bigr| - \mathcal{E}[h\star h^\ast]
\]
forces constraints on the spectral side, and hence on the admissible location of the spectral parameters.

This provides the canonical route by which trace formulae may be used to constrain zeta-type spectra.

\section{Determinant identities and trace expansions}
\label{sec:10.det-trace}

\subsection{Formal determinant expansion}
\label{subsec:10.det.formal}

Let $K$ be a trace class operator on $H$.
Then one has the classical identity
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:10.det.id}
\log\det(I-K)
=
-\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{1}{n}\Tr(K^n),
\end{equation}
where the series converges absolutely for $\|K\|<1$ and extends by analytic continuation in many contexts.

In trace formula applications, one often constructs a family $K(s)$ such that $\Tr(K(s)^n)$ can be expressed
geometrically (for instance, as an orbital integral sum), and the resulting determinant is identified with a zeta
function.

\subsection{Trace-to-zeta mechanism}
\label{subsec:10.det.mechanism}

A typical trace-to-zeta mechanism proceeds as follows.
Assume that there exists an operator family $K(s)$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}
\item for $\Re(s)\gg 1$, $K(s)$ is trace class and $\|K(s)\|<1$;
\item for each $n\ge 1$, the trace $\Tr(K(s)^n)$ admits a geometric expansion of the form
\[
\Tr(K(s)^n)
=
\sum_{\{\gamma\}}
c_n(\gamma)\,e^{-s\ell(\gamma)};
\]
\item the geometric coefficients satisfy the multiplicative compatibility that allows reorganization of the
right-hand side into a logarithmic derivative.
\end{enumerate}
Then \eqref{eq:10.det.id} yields an Euler product representation
\[
\det(I-K(s))
=
\prod_{\{\gamma\}}
\exp\!\left(
-\sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{1}{n}c_n(\gamma)e^{-s\ell(\gamma)}
\right),
\]
and in favorable cases the right-hand side coincides with a zeta-type function.

The relevance to the present work is that the normalized trace framework provides a stable method for producing the
input traces $\Tr(K(s)^n)$ in a way that is insensitive to auxiliary modifications, up to explicit identity
corrections and a quantitative error ledger.

\section{Concluding remarks}
\label{sec:10.conclusion}

The results of this chapter provide the analytic interfaces needed to transport the normalized trace formalism into
zeta-type objects.  The key structural points are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item determinant-level objects can be reconstructed from trace information provided the trace input is stabilized
by normalization;
\item explicit formulae are naturally expressed in terms of normalized trace functionals, with the remaining
noncanonical contributions confined to explicitly controlled terms;
\item positivity criteria arise canonically from convolution squares and yield the natural analytic mechanism for
Li--Weil type constraints on spectral parameters.
\end{enumerate}

In the subsequent concluding chapter, we summarize the normalization mechanism, the stability theorems, and the
resulting canonical trace-to-zeta bridge, and we record the remaining analytic tasks required for full
implementation in specific geometric settings.
