\begin{abstract}
We develop a localized trace formula on finite-area hyperbolic surfaces with cusps. 
A smooth spectral projector onto a dyadic window $[\lambda-\eta,\lambda+\eta]$ is realized microlocally via a compactly supported wave packet, yielding a geometric expansion over closed geodesics of length $\ll \log\lambda$ together with an explicit, power-saving remainder depending only on cusp geometry and a fixed spectral gap. Consequences include a quantitative local Weyl law and variance bounds for Hecke--Maass coefficients in depth. The appendix suite provides effective cusp geometry (A), auxiliary operator and oscillatory estimates (B), a microlocal toolkit (C), Tauberian upgrades (D), numerical verification protocol (E), implementation notes (F), normalization ledger (G), and a comparison with the classical Selberg trace formula (H).
\end{abstract}
