  \begin{abstract}
  We develop a localized trace formula on finite-area hyperbolic surfaces with cusps.
  A smooth spectral projector onto $[\lambda-\eta,\lambda+\eta]$ is constructed
  microlocally and yields a geometric expansion over closed geodesics of length
  $\le T\asymp \log\lambda$, with an explicit power-saving remainder depending only
  on cusp geometry and the spectral gap. Applications include a quantitative local
  Weyl law and variance bounds for Hecke--Maass forms in the depth aspect.
  \end{abstract}
