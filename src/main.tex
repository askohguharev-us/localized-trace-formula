    \small
    \textbf{Abstract.}\;
    We develop a localized trace formalism for finite-area hyperbolic surfaces
    $M=\Gamma\backslash\mathbb{H}$ with cusps, treating the trace formula as a stable
    object under admissible microlocal modifications.  A normalization layer
    canonicalizes the identity sector and consolidates non-canonicity into an explicit
    error ledger.  In short spectral windows $[\lambda-\eta,\lambda+\eta]$ down to
    $\eta=\lambda^{-\theta}$, we obtain power-saving remainders and derive invariant
    spectral consequences, including a quantitative local Weyl law, localized variance
    bounds, and trace-to-zeta interfaces compatible with determinant identities and
    positivity mechanisms.
  \end{tcolorbox}
